Frequently Asked Questioins

  • Information about blood and its importance.

    Blood is a cellular organelle made up of the tangible components of cells (red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets) and plasma. The main role of the blood is to provide nutrients and structure to the organs as well as to remove waste products in the body's metabolism such as carbon dioxide and lactic acid.

  • How and why they should donate blood?

    Donating blood increases calorie burning and aids in weight loss. It is estimated that each donation of 450 ml of blood helps to burn about 650 calories in the body and reduce blood cholesterol. This is a useful measure in weight loss in people with an above-average body weight.

  • What are the conditions for blood donation?

    Healthy people, completely voluntary blood donation.
    Age: 18-60 years old.
    Weight: ≥ 42 kg for women and ≥ 45 kg for men. The amount of blood donated each time should not exceed 9ml/kg of body weight.

    Hemoglobin: 120 g/l Not infected or not infected with HIV and other blood-borne diseases (hepatitis B virus, hepatitis C virus, syphilis...).
    Last donated blood 12 weeks ago or last donated blood components 3 weeks ago.
    Women who are not pregnant or do not raise children under 1 year old.
    Bring one of the identification documents: ID card, citizen identification card, passport, driver's license ...
    Healthy people donate no more than 9 ml/kg body weight each time. People weighing 45 - 50 kg can donate 350 ml of whole blood, people from 50 kg or more can donate 450 ml of whole blood.

  • Are their any side effects of Blood donations ?

    There are no side effects of blood donation. The Blood bank staff ensures that your blood donation is a good experience as far as possible to enable you to become a repeat and a regular blood donor. There are a number of people who have donated more tha25-100 times in their life time.

  • Can I get any disease like AIDS or Hepatitis or any other disease by Blood donation ?

    As only sterile disposables are used to collect blood and these disposables can be used only one time it eliminates any chances of acquiring any disease from blood donation.

  • How often can I donate Blood ?

    After every three –four months you can donate blood.

  • What happens to blood I donate ?

    Your blood in the blood bank is processed and within 6 hours of Blood collection It is separated into components viz. Red Cells, White Cells, Plasma & Platelets. These Blood components are made available for the patients. By making blood components, all the useful parts of blood can be used and from one unit of blood four patients can be benefited.

    Post Donation care -Care that a donor should take after donating blood.

    1. Eat and Drink the refreshment provided before leaving the blood bank
    2. Drink more liquids than usual in next 4 hours
    3. Avoid consuming alcohol until you have eaten something
    4. Don't smoke for next 30 minutes
    5. Avoid climbing stairs for next 30 minutes
    6. If there is bleeding from the phlebotomy site, raise the arm and apply pressure
    7. If fainting or dizziness occurs, either lie down or sit with the head between the knees

  • What should I eat before blood-donation ?

    You will be asked to fill a donor form. Your medical history will be taken by a medical personnel and a small drop of blood to check blood group as well as Hb for anemia. After simple medical screening process and being found medically fit to donate, you will be sent to blood collection room. The actual blood collection from the donor takes about 7-10 minutes followed by a little rest and refreshments.

  • How much time does it take to donate blood ?

    After every three –four months you can donate blood.
